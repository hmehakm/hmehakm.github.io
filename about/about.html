Background

Business roots.
Human-centred practice.

My path into UX research started somewhere unexpected — in business. I spent years understanding how organisations think, how decisions get made, and what drives real outcomes. That foundation shaped the way I approach research today: not just as a method, but as a strategic lever.

I've completed my Master's in Human-Computer Interaction at DePaul University, Chicago — deepening a practice that sits at the intersection of people, systems, and design. It's given rigour to my instincts and language to what I was already doing intuitively.

What excites me most is the space I occupy: the cusp of UX research and product & project management. I understand research as a product in itself — with timelines, stakeholders, and deliverables that need to move in step with the teams around them. I bring agile thinking into every project I work on, running research in sprints, aligning on scope early, and keeping findings actionable throughout the product lifecycle.
